🪳 Cross-Site-Scripting (XSS) detection

Cross-site scripting (XSS) is a security vulnerability that allows attackers to inject malicious scripts into web-apps.

These scripts are typically executed in the user's browser, enabling the attacker to perform actions such as stealing cookies, defacing websites, or redirecting users to malicious sites.

📬 Email-Phishing detection

Email phishing is a cyber attack where attackers send deceptive emails to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords or financial details.

These emails often mimic legitimate organizations or contacts and create a sense of urgency to prompt the recipient to click on malicious links or download harmful attachments.

🔑 Password strength analyzer

Password strength refers to how resistant a password is to being guessed or cracked by attackers.

A strong password typically includes a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters.
It should also be at least 12 characters long and avoid common words or easily guessable patterns.
